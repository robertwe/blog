Hi there, my name is Robert and I'm a husband, father of three and a software developer for over 20 years now.
I love to automate things, programming and clouds (computing clouds - for sure :) ).
At my work I follow the path of the Software Craftsman (at least I do my best).
Who is that, you can ask? In my opinion _Micah Martin_ described it perfectly in
[this post](http://blog.8thlight.com/micah-martin/2008/09/21/definition-of-software-craftsman.html).
I have been doing and promoting DevOps culture since 2011, and working remotely for just as long.
I started my career as a developer, later moved to operations, and these days I work where those two meet.

My day-to-day toolbox:

  - [_Go_](https://go.dev/) - my language of choice since 2018, mostly backend APIs, Kubernetes operators
    and command line tools,
  - [_Kubernetes_](https://kubernetes.io/) - running production workloads on it for years now
    (this blog started its life on a K8s cluster too),
  - [_Terraform_](https://www.terraform.io/) / [_OpenTofu_](https://opentofu.org/) - an early Terraform adopter
    (since ~2015), recently migrated the environments I work with to OpenTofu,
  - [_AWS_](https://aws.amazon.com/) - years of hands-on experience: Lambda, EC2, EKS, S3, Route53,
    CloudWatch and friends,
  - [_Python_](https://www.python.org/) - my go-to for scripts and glue code, a lot of it running as AWS Lambdas,
  - CI/CD - GitHub Actions and Jenkins (including automating Jenkins itself - DSL, pipelines),
  - [_Podman_](https://podman.io/) / [_OrbStack_](https://orbstack.dev/) and containers in general.

In the past I also spent serious time with PHP (Laravel, Symfony), Node.js, SaltStack, Packer and Serverless
Framework - they all taught me something, even if they are no longer in daily rotation.
